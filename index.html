What is Outdoor Ambient Air Pollution?

Ambient air pollution is when a population is exposed to PM 2.5, No2, and O3 at low exposures for long periods of time.

The EPA identified and actively measures 6 common air pollutants.

Carbon Monoxide (CO): released from mainly car, truck, and other vehicle emissions.

Sulfer Dioxide (SO2): released from mainly burning fossil fuels in industry facilities.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2): released from vehicle emissions and burning fossil fuels.

Lead (Pb): released into the air from metal processing plants, waste incinerators, and aircrafts using leaded aviation fuel.

Ground-Level Ozone: created through reactions of nitrogen oxides, VOCs, and sunlight.

Particulate Matter: released from sources like fires & construction sites or formed through chemical reactions of pollutants above.

These air pollutant particles are SMALL!

Fine Beach Sand: 90 microns in diameter
Human Hair: 50-70 microns in diameter.
Dust, pollen, mold: less than 10 microns in diameter
**Ambient Pollutants: less than 2.5 microns

Because we inhale ambient pollutants without even knowing it, [researchers] are actively measuring these invisible pollutants.
