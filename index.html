What is Outdoor Ambient Air Pollution?

Ambient air pollution is when a population is exposed to PM 2.5, No2, and O3 at low exposures for long periods of time.

These ambient air pollutants can come from:

Car exhaust releasing carbon particles

Agriculture releasing nitrates from fertilizers and dust from field work

Wildfires and dust storms releasing carbon particles, nitrate particles, and dust (AKA tiny-tiny rocks)

These particles are SMALL!

Fine Beach Sand: 90 microns in diameter
Human Hair: 50-70 microns in diameter.
Dust, pollen, mold: less than 10 microns in diameter
**Ambient Pollutants: less than 2.5 microns

Because we inhale ambient pollutants without even knowing it, [researchers] are actively measuring these invisible pollutants.
