Outdoor Ambient Air Pollution and Health Consquences

What is Outdoor Ambient Air Pollution?

Ambient air pollution occurs when a population is exposed to pollutants at LOW exposures for LONG periods of time.

The EPA identified and actively measures 6 common air pollutants.

Carbon Monoxide (CO)

released from mainly car, truck, and other vehicle emissions.

Sulfer Dioxide (SO2)

released from mainly burning fossil fuels in industry facilities.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2)

released from vehicle emissions and burning fossil fuels.

Lead (Pb)

released into the air from metal processing plants, waste incinerators, and aircrafts using leaded aviation fuel.

Ground-Level Ozone (O3)

created through reactions of nitrogen oxides, VOCs, and sunlight.
--

Particulate Matter (PM)

released from sources like fires & construction sites or formed through chemical reactions of pollutants.

Where do these pollutants come from?

These air pollutant particles are SMALL!

Fine Beach Sand: 90 microns in diameter
Human Hair: 50-70 microns in diameter.
Dust, pollen, mold: less than 10 microns in diameter
**Ambient Pollutants: less than 2.5 microns

Because we inhale ambient pollutants without even knowing it, [researchers] are actively measuring these invisible pollutants.
